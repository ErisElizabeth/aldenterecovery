Meeting Script

Chairperson: Welcome, everyone, to the Friday night "Syntax & Sauce" meeting of Al Dente Recovery. My name is [Name], I'm a developer, and I'm your Quartermaster for tonight.

Let's open with a moment of silence for anyone still stuck in an infinite loop, followed by the Refactoring Request.

Group: Grant us the logic to accept the legacy code we cannot change, The focus to rewrite the modules we can, And the wisdom to know when to just restart the server. R'amen.

Chairperson: Al Dente Recovery is a fellowship of individuals who share their experience, logic, and garlic bread with each other so that they may solve their common bugs and help others to recover from substance use.

We are not allied with any sect, denomination, politics, or organization; mostly because we don't like unnecessary dependencies. Our primary purpose is to stay firm but flexible, and to help others build their own framework for a balanced life.

For those new here, we do not require you to surrender your will to a Higher Power. You are the admin of your own system. The Flying Spaghetti Monster is simply a reminder not to take dogma too seriously, and that sometimes life is just a bowl of tangled noodles that takes time to sort out.

Let's go around the room and introduce ourselves. Feel free to share your name, your pronouns, your favorite pasta shape, and whether your personal server is currently running smoothly or experiencing a little downtime. I'll start: I'm [Name], she/her, cavatappi, and I'm currently running a stable build today.

(Introductions go around the room)

Chairperson: Thank you. At this point in the meeting, we usually read one of the Eight "I'd Really Rather You Didn'ts" from the Loose Canon. Would someone like to read number four?"

Volunteer: I'd really rather you didn't view a relapse as a catastrophic moral failing that resets your progress to zero. Sometimes the pasta sticks to the bottom of the pot; you just scrape it off, adjust the heat, and keep cooking.

Chairperson: Thank you. We don't track so-called "clean time" with a ticking clock that you lose if you encounter an error. Every day you wrote good code counts, even if the program crashed on Tuesday.

Tonight's topic is "Creativity as a Patch." A lot of us found that when we stopped drinking or using, there was a massive vacuum in our processing power. For me, learning to code became the new syntax for my life. It gave me structure, line by line, when everything else felt chaotic. The floor is now open for anyone who wants to share how they've replaced old, buggy habits with new, creative frameworks.

No cross-talk, no unsolicited debugging of other people's lives. Just share your own repository.

(Sharing portion of the meeting takes place)

Chairperson: That's all the time we have for sharing. Thank you to everyone who contributed their logs tonight.

Before we close, we're going to pass the colander around. We have no dues or fees, but we do have to pay for the domain hosting, the coffee, and the garlic bread. Give if you can, don't stress if you can't.

If you are experiencing a runtime error tonight and feel like you might crash, please reach out to someone before you leave. We don't have formal sponsors here, but there are plenty of senior devs in the room willing to do some pair programming with you.

Let's circle up, hold hands or link elbows if you're comfortable, and close out.

Group: May our code be clean, May our boundaries be firm, And may we always remember that we hold the keys to our own root access. R'amen!
